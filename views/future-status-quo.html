This scenario relates the status quo, the current state of library technology collaboration in Colorado and extrapolates existing trends with no significant changes from the current moment.

Public, K-12 media centers, Academic, and Special Libraries participate in formal and informal collaborations with similar and different libraries in a variety of consortiums including CLiC,, Marmot, and CARL. This collaborative include shared ILS (Marmot & AspenCat), shared digital repositories (CARL), shared website hosting (Plinkit), and a shared union catalog (AspenCat, Prospector, Marmot).

In 2018 many of these library consortiums will use a variety of commercial and open-source technologies to deliver library services. We will see more experimentation in library technology collaboration especially between libraries that share geographic proximity, type (public, academic, special, or K-12), or other shared characteristic or other shared characteristics. Most library platforms and services will be delivered seamless over the even more persistent network to a wider range of devices including wrist-watch and eye-glasses form factors. Finally, in 2018 hybrid environments where resources like books and articles are available both in print and in electronic formats, will be available in ever greater numbers with the same messy mix of licensing, copyrights, platforms, DRAM, and availability.

We expect that funding for libraries will remain the same or with slight increases.
