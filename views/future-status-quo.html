Leadership Questions

  • How would you answer the question, "What will library technology collaboration in Colorado look like in 2018?" given this scenario
  • Are the current library technology collaborations particularly through library consortiums acceptable to you and your institution?
  • What choices and leadership can we build upon in our current library technology that would position us for us to achieve even this, the status quo future?

This scenario relates the status quo, the current state of library technology collaboration in Colorado and extrapolates existing trends with no significant changes from the current time in the Fall of 2013.

Public, K-12 media centers, academic, and special Libraries participate in formal and informal collaborations with similar and different libraries in a variety of consortiums including CLiC,, Marmot, and CARL. These collaborations include the following library technologies:

  • shared ILS (Marmot & AspenCat)
  • shared digital repositories (Alliance Digital Repository)
  • shared website hosting (Plinkit)
  • shared union catalog (AspenCat, Prospector, Marmot).

In 2018 many of these library consortiums will use a variety of commercial and open-source technologies to deliver the equivalent of our current library services. We will see more experimentation in library technology collaboration especially between libraries that share geographic proximity, type (public, academic, special, or K-12), or other shared characteristic. Most library platforms and services will be delivered seamless over the even more persistent network to a wider range of devices including wrist-watch and eye-glasses form factors. Finally, in 2018 hybrid environments where resources like books and articles are available both in print and in electronic formats, will be available in ever greater numbers with the same messy mix of licensing, copyrights, platforms, digital rights management, and availability that we experience today.

We expect that funding for libraries will remain the same or with slight increases.
