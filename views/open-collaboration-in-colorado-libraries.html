Open Collaboration in Colorado Libraries

My goal with the development of the Redis Library Services Platform is that of a pull platform that is simple to use by the patrons, is scalable, stable, an affordable for libraries and other memory institutions to support. The platform allows libraries to build single-page HTML5 apps that leverage the best-of-class open-source web and data storage technologies.

At Colorado College, the current development path of the Redis Library Services Platform is primarily about automating workflows for our ebook and electronic resources cataloging along with ingestion and management of our digital repository.

What's Next?

Library technology collaboration in Colorado should include some discussions on how to better position all of our libraries as we prepare for five years in the future.

I am very interested in working with non-academic library in creating an HTML5 App to address a challenge you might have in your library. I will be using techniques and processes modified from the Lean Startup and Lean Manufacturing. A central tenet of the Lean Startup approach is to build a minimum viable product that then is improved in iterative process with direct feedback and responding to the demands of the end user.

Partners in Open Source Development

With the drop in price and with more options for technology hosting, small and medium organizations have more opportunities than ever before in disrupting and embracing new cloud services that can deliver more services on more devices. The Redis Library Services Platform offers such a technology and by partnering up with a non-academic library in the development and release of an app, we can improve both the platform and the profession through collaborative technology development under a lean open-source model of iterative development.
