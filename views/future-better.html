Starting Scenario

With the relative economic boom of most counties in Colorado along with the recognition among local communities about the value of public and academic libraries in their communities technological collaboration among libraries flourishes with the adoption of open-source bibliographic services running on what they would call a hybrid cloud in 2013. Many larger libraries offer Makerspaces that offer free or reduced costs access to their patrons to build and use 3D printers, Ardiunos, Raspberry Pi, and other open hardware platforms for projects and experimentation that create whole new categories of materials that need cataloging, curating managing, and publishing. Libraries because a central hub of sharing in their communities of original and remixed creative

As a response to the increased wildfire danger, many Colorado Libraries in communities that are likely to be impacted will offer emergency information services to distressed citizens. They accomplish this through a resilient library technology pull platform and mobilizes citizens across the state to share resources and information when emergencies like wildfires or floods.

Library technologies services are quickly built and pushed out across the various formal and informal networks of library services including formal library consortium as well as ad hoc sharing networks without any formal organizational structures. Library collections are widely with access through responsive web apps that morph and configure themselves automatically to the changing device forms including all the smartphones, tablets, augmented glasses, wristwatches, and multiple and proliferating screens.
