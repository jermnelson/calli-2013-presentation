Starting Scenario

Libraries are the center of new revolution in shareable, open, and aggressively local community centers that focus on cataloging, sharing, and promoting the virtual and physical artifacts created by their communities. Cheap 3D Printing, coupled with significant copyright reform by the United States, has created an explosion of creative remixing of intellectual and creative works. Librarians are firmly grounded in the idea of libraries being a magical place of discovery and learning as they embrace a much more inclusive definition of collections. These augmented spaces start with merging and growth of smart, embedded objects that draw design inspiration from the ancient and renaissance memory theatres that offer a whole suite of interaction opportunities and enhancements that seem magical to the particpants Artifactsin these libraries span both the physical and digital with powerful manifestations that compliment each other.

With the growth and proliferation of small and large datasets, generated by pervasive sensors, video feeds, and people's actions, including for most people, a basic genetic profile, they are starting to be concerned about the permanence and legacy of these data-sets. Libraries, recognizing this as an extension of their existing services and philosophy; collect, share, and curate these small-to-midsize data sets. These services start by collaborating with other libraries and memory institutions to offer an open-standards life-vault service that allows patrons to archive and preserve these datasets with options to share as much as little with the world and their community.

Libraries share their collections and their technologies in whole new ways to support these roles. Libraries connect both laterally with local, regional, state-wide libraries and also horizontally within their communities by becoming the center for these new creative activities and actively manage the creative artifacts, including supporting data sets, of their communities.
